Decolonial AI Literature Review

Toward Decolonial Educational AI: A Systematic Review

Research Purpose

This PRISMA-compliant systematic literature review examines theoretical frameworks, empirical evidence, and practical applications for decolonial approaches to AI in education grounded in African, Indigenous, and Black radical pedagogical traditions.

Key Research Questions

  • How do African-centered pedagogies inform educational AI design?
  • What Indigenous West African epistemologies offer alternative frameworks for AI systems?
  • How does critical pedagogy and Black radical tradition shape decolonial AI praxis?
  • What measurement frameworks exist that challenge colonial logics in educational AI?

The Right Path Framework

A Decolonial Framework for Educational AI

Framework Vision

The Right Path presents an original conceptual framework that reimagines educational AI systems through lenses grounded in African, Indigenous, and Black radical pedagogies rather than dominant Western paradigms.

Five Pillars

  • Epistemic Grounding & Ancestral Knowledge: Sankofa, African fractals, oral tradition as epistemic technology
  • Relational Ethics & Collective Intelligence: Ubuntu philosophy, communal knowledge building, collaborative AI design
  • Critical Consciousness & Structural Literacy: Conscientização, algorithmic literacy, awareness of algorithmic bias
  • Cultural Continuity, Creativity & Expressive Freedom: Culturally Sustaining Pedagogy, Black expressive culture, creative resistance
  • Liberation, Agency & Human Flourishing: Abolitionist teaching, economic sovereignty, futures orientation

Three-Layer Architecture

The framework operates across three interconnected layers: Pedagogical (teaching and learning practices), Technological (AI system design and functionality), and Governance (policy, accountability, and oversight).

AI Flourishing Readiness Index

Measurement Instrument for Decolonial AI Assessment

Instrument Purpose

A psychometrically validated 80-point assessment tool designed to measure institutional readiness for implementing decolonial educational AI across five critical domains aligned with The Right Path framework.

Five Assessment Domains

  • Epistemic Grounding (16 pts): Sankofa integration, African computational thinking, oral tradition technologies
  • Relational Ethics (16 pts): Ubuntu philosophy, communal knowledge building, collaborative AI design
  • Critical Consciousness (16 pts): Algorithmic literacy, awareness of bias, structural analysis
  • Cultural Continuity (16 pts): Culturally Sustaining Pedagogy practices, Black expressive culture, creative resistance
  • Liberation & Flourishing (16 pts): Abolitionist teaching, economic sovereignty, futures orientation

Decolonial AI Practitioner Toolkit

Implementation Tools and Vendor Audit Framework

Purpose

Provides K-12 educators, administrators, and EdTech leaders with practical, actionable tools for implementing decolonial AI frameworks in their schools and districts, including a comprehensive evaluation of 50-100 educational AI vendors.

Five Implementation Phases

  • Planning & Preparation: Readiness assessment, stakeholder mapping, vision statement development
  • Team Formation: Committee structure, role definition, professional development planning
  • Pilot Implementation: Small-scale testing, data collection, iterative feedback loops
  • District-Wide Rollout: Communication strategy, policy integration, resource allocation
  • Sustainability: Continuous improvement, ongoing vendor evaluation, knowledge management

Vendor Audit Framework

Comprehensive 100-point evaluation system assessing vendors across five criteria: Functionality & Features (30 pts), Cost & Value (20 pts), Support & Training (20 pts), Integration & Interoperability (15 pts), and Equity & Accessibility (15 pts).

African & Indigenous Pedagogies

Deep Theoretical Foundations Series

Series Vision

A collection of five focused theoretical articles that provide deep philosophical and epistemological grounding for The Right Path framework, drawing from African-centered, Indigenous West African, and Black radical pedagogical traditions.

Five Article Series

  • Culturally Relevant & Sustaining Pedagogy: Ladson-Billings, Paris, Alim, Muhammad—How do CRP/CSP/HRL frameworks inform decolonial AI design?
  • Afrocentricity & African-Centered Education: Asante, Hilliard, King, Woodson—What role does epistemic sovereignty play in educational AI?
  • Indigenous West African Epistemologies: Sankofa, Ubuntu, Ifá, African Fractals—How do Indigenous knowledge systems reshape AI architectures?
  • Critical Pedagogy & Liberation: Freire, hooks, Giroux, Black Radical Tradition—What does critical consciousness mean in the age of AI?
  • Decolonial AI Scholarship: Birhane, Benjamin, Noble, Browne, Mohamed—How do we operationalize decolonial AI in educational contexts?

Scholarly Impact

Each article engages deeply with primary sources, offers critical analysis of strengths and limitations, and makes explicit connections to educational AI applications, advancing both theoretical understanding and practical implementation.

NSF Grant Proposal

Decolonial AI for Educational Equity

Grant Vision

A comprehensive 3-5 year research program to develop, implement, and validate The Right Path decolonial AI framework across diverse K-12 educational settings, promoting equity, human flourishing, and culturally sustaining learning in the Fourth Industrial Revolution.

Five Specific Aims

  • Aim 1: Refine and validate The Right Path framework through participatory design with educators and communities
  • Aim 2: Develop and pilot The Right Path AI & Human Flourishing Readiness Index for institutional assessment
  • Aim 3: Create comprehensive practitioner toolkit with evaluation of 50-100 educational AI vendors
  • Aim 4: Conduct multi-site implementation study across diverse educational contexts
  • Aim 5: Disseminate findings through publications, professional development, and policy engagement

Broader Impacts

This research program will advance NSF's goals for broadening participation in STEM, advancing discovery while promoting teaching, training, and learning, and enhancing the well-being of individuals and society by centering equity in educational AI systems.
